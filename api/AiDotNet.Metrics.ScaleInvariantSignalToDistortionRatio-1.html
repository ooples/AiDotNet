Remarks

SI-SDR is the standard metric for evaluating source separation quality. It measures how well the estimated signal matches the target signal, ignoring scale differences.

Formula: SI-SDR = 10 * log10(||s_target||^2 / ||e_noise||^2) where s_target is the projection of the estimate onto the target, and e_noise is the residual.

Higher values indicate better separation quality. Typical values: - >15 dB: Excellent separation - 10-15 dB: Good separation - 5-10 dB: Fair separation - <5 dB: Poor separation
