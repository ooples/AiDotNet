Returns

T

The L2 norm of the vector.

Remarks

For Beginners: The L2 norm is the "length" or "magnitude" of a vector in a mathematical sense. It's calculated by taking the square root of the sum of squares of all elements. For example, the L2 norm of [3,4] is sqrt(3^2 + 4^2) = sqrt(9 + 16) = sqrt(25) = 5. This is commonly used in machine learning to measure the "size" of vectors or the distance between points.
