Remarks

Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) factors a matrix A into the product A = U * S * V^T, where U and V are orthogonal matrices and S is a diagonal matrix containing singular values. SVD is one of the most important matrix decompositions with applications across many fields of science and engineering.

For Beginners: SVD is a way to break down a matrix into three simpler matrices (U, S, and V^T). Think of it like factoring a number, but for matrices. This decomposition is useful for data compression, noise reduction, and solving systems of equations.

Real-world applications: - Data compression and dimensionality reduction (PCA) - Image compression and denoising - Recommender systems (collaborative filtering)
