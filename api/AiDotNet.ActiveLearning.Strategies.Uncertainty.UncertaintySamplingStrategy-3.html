Remarks

For Beginners: Uncertainty sampling is the simplest and most popular active learning strategy. It selects samples where the model is most uncertain about its prediction.

Uncertainty Measures:

  • Entropy: H(p) = -Î£ p_i * log(p_i) - uncertainty across all classes
  • Least Confidence: 1 - max(p) - uncertainty in top prediction
  • Margin: p_1 - p_2 - difference between top two predictions

When to Use:

  • Good baseline for most classification problems
  • Fast to compute (single forward pass)
  • Works well when model uncertainty correlates with true difficulty

Reference: Lewis and Gale "A Sequential Algorithm for Training Text Classifiers" (1994)
