Remarks

For Beginners: Autocorrelation refers to the correlation of a time series with its own past values. In the context of model fitting, autocorrelation in residuals (prediction errors) can indicate that the model is missing important patterns in the data.

This detector uses the Durbin-Watson statistic to measure autocorrelation in model residuals. The Durbin-Watson statistic ranges from 0 to 4, with a value of 2 indicating no autocorrelation, values less than 2 indicating positive autocorrelation, and values greater than 2 indicating negative autocorrelation.

Remarks

For Beginners: This method calculates the Durbin-Watson statistic from your model's errors and interprets it to determine what type of autocorrelation (if any) is present.

The Durbin-Watson statistic ranges from 0 to 4:

  • Values near 2 (typically 1.5-2.5) indicate no autocorrelation
  • Values below 1.5 suggest positive autocorrelation (errors tend to be followed by errors of the same sign)
  • Values above 2.5 suggest negative autocorrelation (errors tend to be followed by errors of the opposite sign)
